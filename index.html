What This Means

Grade A (85-100)

Clear terms, minimal data collection, strong user rights. Rare.

Grade B (70-84)

Mostly fair terms with some questionable clauses. Transparent about what they collect.

Grade C (50-69)

Industry standard — which means not great. Data selling, forced arbitration, content licensing.

Grade D (30-49)

Aggressively anti-consumer. Broad data rights, impossible opt-outs, retroactive changes.

Grade F (0-29)

Predatory. Treats your data like theirs. Designed to extract maximum value from you.

Why This Matters More Than Ever

Within 12-18 months, most people will have AI agents acting on their behalf — booking, buying, signing up, negotiating. Those agents will hand out your personal data hundreds of times a day.

A human might agree to 5 terms of service per week. Your agent will do 50 per day. Without a sovereignty layer between your agent and the world, the privacy problem doesn't get better — it becomes catastrophic.

We're building a solution.

A browser extension that reads terms before you agree. Scores them. Flags conflicts with your personal policy. Alerts you when they change after you've agreed. And gives you a kill switch for every connection.
